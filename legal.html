What is Legal Aid ?

Legal aid is the provision of assistance to people
otherwise unable to afford legal representation
and access to the court system. Legal aid is
regarded as central in providing access to justice
by ensuring

  • Equality before the Law
  • The Right to Counsel
  • The Right to a Fair Trial

Free Legal Aid

Legal Aid is the Professional Assistance to the People who are unable to
afford Legal Documentation and Access to the Court of Law. Legal Aid is a
Professional Mechanism to provide service to everyone to access the
Justice by ensuring Equality before the Law, Right to have Counsel and the
Right to a Fair Trial. Article 14 of the Constitution of India Defines 'Equality
before the Law and Equal Protection by the Law'. Article 39A of the
Constitution of India, provides for Equal Justice and Free Legal Aid:

The 'Connect To Justice' always Committed to Secure that the operation of
the Legal System Promotes Prompt Justice on the basis of Equal
Opportunity to Access Justice for Everyone by providing Free Legal Aid to
the Financially Weak and Distress People. It is our vision and mission to
provide Free Legal Aid for the Ends of Justice. Any Person should not be
Deprived and Denied to Access Justice for any reason of Financial or any
other Disabilities.

The Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 made drastic changes in the field of
Free Legal Aid. It is an Act to constitute legal services authorities to provide
free and competent legal services to the weaker sections of the society to
ensure that opportunities for securing justice are not denied to any citizen
by reason of economic or other disabilities.

We provide Awareness to the People about their Rights through our Free
Legal Aid Camp and render Professional Services to Protect it without any
Discrimination of Financial or any other Disabilities.
